History


[photograph of aurora skyline over the Fox River]. (n.d.).https://d1ja9tyo8nbkbc.cloudfront.net/47687352_S0412/S0412/S0412-R0100/MRD11908209/MRD11908209-2.jpg?version=1697820627&width=640

Aurora was first settled in 1834. It was later incorporated in 1845. In 1913, Aurora absorbed an adjacent municipality adding to its size. Aurora was built on the Fox river which allowed for an easy power source for mills and access to maritime trade. Aurora began to boom courtesy of the industrial revolution and the factories and technologies that came with it. These factories benefited from rivers with strong currents (like the Fox River) for power.

Population

Aurora has a population of 180,542. This makes it the second largest city in Illinois. Aurora's population is diverse as it relied heavily on the influx of immigrants for labor in the factories.

Classification:

  • Despite the large population, Aurora is suburban as it is a suburb of Chicago.

Geography

Aurora is located in northeastern Illinois. It is approximately 40 miles west of Chicago. Aurora is located on the Fox River that runs through the city. The downtown area of Aurora is located on an island in the middle of the Fox River. The city's terrain is, like most of Illinois, flat and was once a large prairie.

Income

  • The median household income in Aurora is $84,578.
  • This is higher than the $78,443 median household income for the state.
  • Aurora's median income is 7.8% higher than that of the state's.
