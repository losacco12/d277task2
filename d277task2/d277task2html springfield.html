History


Terry Farmer. (2010). "State Capitol Springfield, Illinois". [photograph]. http://www.ilstatehouse.com/images/t_farmer.jpg

People began inhabiting springfield in 1820, but it was not incorporated until 1821. It was originally named Calhoun after John C. Calhoun. This name was changed to Springfield, named after Spring Creek that flows through the Northwest of the town, in 1832. Springfield was made the capitol of Illinois in 1839 and has served as the state's capital ever since. The city served as president Abraham Lincoln's primary residence for the majority of his life.

Population

Springfield, Illinois had a population of 112,544 as of the 2020 census. The city also has a metropolitan area with a cumulative population of around 208,000 people as of the 2020 census.

Classification:

  • As Illinois' Capitol city and a top 10 largest city in the state, Springfield is classified as urban.

Geography

Springfield is located in central Illinois. It is located on a plain within a valley and is at on the edge of tornado alley despite rarely seeing tornadoes. It features the Spring Creek and Lake Springfield.
